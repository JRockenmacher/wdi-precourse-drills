Who Am I?

Neither am I a girl, from a small town, and my world sure isn't lonely (especially in Denver). My name is Jean-Scott "JR" Rockenmacher and I want to create a career in coding. I grew up in the north end of San Diego County in the city of Carlsbad. I was always a creative weirdo, but so are my parents and I do not see it as a bad thing, as it has shaped both my personality and cognition. Growing up wasn't terrible but being a brazen, unique individual in a society that values artifice did net me all the benefits of one who operates against the grain. With my upbringing and the local societal experience, I risked moving to denver for a semester at a school for prospecive protestant missionaries. I attended a second semester, after which, I decided to stay. I fell in love with the compact nature of the metro area, the warmth of its people, and the cultural and recreational ammenities it had. I "grew-up" in my 20's here, and became the person I wanted to be. The journey hasn't been easy though: as intelligent and talented as I am with a variety of things, I have learned (still learning) how to manage life and finances and make choices that give me the most freedom to pursue the life I want. My friends I have collected as a result of the personal growth I went through in my 20's have pushed me to find a career to afford me the freedom to pursue the life I want and that is mentally stimulating to be satisfied and challenged with my work.

Since moving out here in 2005, I have picked up a few hobbies.

  • Racing and tuning cars
  • Camping with as little ammenities possibel
  • Challenging myself to create new recipes that marry delicious food with healthy, humane, and sustainbly sourced ingredients
  • Scouring the city for new coffee shops and ethnic foods

All in all, I'm a decent guy trying to live my life intentionally and with deliberate, well-thought-out decisions. I like putting effort into all that I plan to do, so I can make the most out of any project I undertake. I continue to grow in the liberal arts to keep myself well rounded and mentally flexible. Till jumping into coding, I didn't think I would ever find a calling, but how enthusiastic I am about learning and implement what I learn, I ventue to put my hopes into the possibility web-development may be it. Which brings me to where I am today!
